    My Story
    The story is still going on ...

    Fall 2019
        My major was energy and power engineering (Engineering Thermophysics). With the ample time, I started to learn the C language that I used to learn in high school, and solved many problems related to experimental data processing through that. It made me realize that my interest is actually in computer science. So I decided to take the major transfer exam to enter the College of Computer Science and Technology. The epidemic during the winter vacation gave me plenty of time to continue my studies on CS. It was also during that period of time that I started to using Linux and learning system-level programming. I conducted a lot of hands-on practice and gained many problem-solving skills.

    Spring 2020
        Due to the epidemic, I took online courses at home. On the condition that the original major courses are guaranteed to be excellent, I continue to study CS. CMU's Course 15-213: Introduction to Computer Systems and the textbook "Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective " have further improved my ability. Also I use my own C language ability to help my classmates learn C language courses.

    Fall 2020
        I passed the major transfer exam and came to the College of Computer Science and Technology. I started a lot of exposure to various directions in this field, and eventually found that I was very interested in operating systems, hardware drivers, and architecture. Also I started to learning assembly and hardware-related knowledge, and started coding operating system kernel. "Advanced Programming in Unix Environment " improved my understanding of Linux and system programming ability.

    Spring 2021
        A lot of my free time was spent on re-learning the first year courses on the College of Computer Science and Technology. In the C language course design, combined multi-process, compiler and database theory, the project was highly praised by the professor. At the same time, the course Microcomputer Systems allowed me to start exploring the x86_64 system. Through "Intel 64 and IA-32 Architectures Software Developer's Manual Volume 3", I had a further understanding of the architecture, and completed the BootLoader through assembly in the initialization stage of the operating system. Also in order to help my roommates preparing the final of Computer Architecture ,I implemented Tomasulo algorithm for simulation.

    Fall 2021
        I spent a lot of time in MIT6.828: Operating System Engineering, completed part of the labs (startup, memory management, process management). Also, in MIT6.824: Distributed Systems, I read part of the classic papers, and developed a strong interest in distributed systems. The courses "GPGPU Heterogeneous High Performance Computing" makes me feel that I have gained a lot. Using CUDA to complete the design of the FFT algorithm, I have a new understanding of HPC.

    Spring 2022
        I started to write my own operating system EOS in assembly and C, and go further in architecture related. At the same time, for distributed systems, I continued to read papers and gradually implemented classic algorithms such as Mapreduce, Raft, etc. During this Spring Festival I figured out what I want to do in the future. When I was an elementary school student, I dreamed to be a scientist (microbiologist). When I was a middle school student, I dreamed to be a scientist (physicist) and, to be honestly, for more than six years that's my original dream. But sometimes things go athwart. Luckily I found my true love Computer Science ! Why not computer scientist?
